Hydraulic Pressure Testing
Services Description

Hydraulic pressure testing is a critical safety procedure performed on fire extinguishers and other pressurized fire safety equipment to ensure they can withstand the operating pressures they may encounter during use. This testing involves filling the extinguisher with water and applying pressure significantly higher than its normal operating pressure to check for leaks, structural integrity, and overall durability. Hydraulic pressure testing is required periodically to comply with safety regulations and standards, ensuring that fire extinguishers are safe and reliable.

Testing Procedure

The testing procedure begins with the extinguisher being emptied and cleaned. It is then filled with water and connected to a hydraulic testing machine. Pressure is gradually increased to a specified level, usually 1.5 times the operating pressure, and held for a predetermined period. The extinguisher is inspected for any signs of leaks, bulging, or deformation. If the extinguisher passes the test, it is emptied, dried, reassembled, and marked with a new test date. If it fails, it is removed from service and replaced.
